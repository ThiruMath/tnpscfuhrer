**Whole Numbers:**

**Definition:**
Whole numbers are a set of numbers that include all the natural numbers (positive integers) along with zero. They are represented by the symbols {0, 1, 2, 3, ...}. Whole numbers do not include fractions or decimals, and they are used to count objects or represent quantities that cannot be divided into smaller parts.

**Characteristics:**
1. **Includes Zero:** Unlike natural numbers, which start from 1, whole numbers include the additional element of zero.
2. **Counting and Cardinality:** Whole numbers are used for counting and cardinality, indicating how many objects or elements are present in a set.
3. **Closure under Addition and Subtraction:** Adding or subtracting any two whole numbers always results in another whole number.
4. **Not Closed under Division:** Division of whole numbers may not always result in a whole number. For example, 5 ÷ 2 = 2.5, which is not a whole number.
5. **Used in Various Contexts:** Whole numbers are used in various fields such as mathematics, finance, science, and everyday life for counting, measuring, and representing quantities.

**Applications:**
1. **Counting Objects:** Whole numbers are used to count objects, people, or any discrete items.
2. **Measurement:** They are used to measure quantities such as length, mass, volume, and time.
3. **Finance:** Whole numbers are used in financial transactions, budgeting, and accounting.
4. **Science:** They are used in scientific calculations and measurements.
