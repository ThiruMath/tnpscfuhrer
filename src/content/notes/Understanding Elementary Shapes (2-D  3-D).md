Certainly! Here are the types of quadrilaterals:

1. **Square:**
   - A square is a quadrilateral with all four sides of equal length and all four angles of \(90^\circ\).
   - Properties:
     - All sides are equal in length.
     - All angles are right angles (\(90^\circ\)).
     - Diagonals are equal in length and bisect each other at right angles.

2. **Rectangle:**
   - A rectangle is a quadrilateral with opposite sides of equal length and all four angles of \(90^\circ\).
   - Properties:
     - Opposite sides are equal in length.
     - All angles are right angles (\(90^\circ\)).
     - Diagonals are equal in length and bisect each other.

3. **Rhombus:**
   - A rhombus is a quadrilateral with all four sides of equal length but opposite angles are not necessarily equal.
   - Properties:
     - All sides are equal in length.
     - Opposite angles are equal.
     - Diagonals bisect each other at right angles.

4. **Parallelogram:**
   - A parallelogram is a quadrilateral with opposite sides parallel and equal in length, and opposite angles equal.
   - Properties:
     - Opposite sides are equal in length.
     - Opposite angles are equal.
     - Diagonals bisect each other.

5. **Trapezoid (Trapezium):**
   - A trapezoid is a quadrilateral with one pair of parallel sides.
   - Properties:
     - One pair of opposite sides are parallel.
     - Non-parallel sides are called legs, and the parallel sides are called bases.
     - Diagonals do not necessarily bisect each other.

6. **Kite:**
   - A kite is a quadrilateral with two distinct pairs of adjacent sides that are equal in length.
   - Properties:
     - Two distinct pairs of adjacent sides are equal in length.
     - Diagonals intersect at right angles.
     - One diagonal bisects the other.

These are the main types of quadrilaterals, each with its own unique properties. Understanding these properties helps in identifying and classifying quadrilaterals in geometry problems.
