## 💰 Money & Currency – Flashcards

Money :: Anything that is generally accepted as a medium of exchange

Silver Tanka :: Early silver coin used during Delhi Sultanate

Sher Shah Suri Rupiyah :: Standardized silver currency introduced by Sher Shah Suri

Full-bodied coins :: Coins whose intrinsic metal value equals face value

Token coins :: Coins whose face value is greater than metal value

Paper currency :: Fiat money

Fiat Money :: Money with no intrinsic value, backed by government authority

Fiat money must be issued by :: Government or Central Bank

Legal Tender :: Money legally acceptable for payment of all debts and obligations

Conditions of Legal Tender :: Must be fiat money + must be valid for all transactions throughout the country

Cheque :: A negotiable instrument directing a bank to pay a specified amount

Demand Draft :: A bank instrument for money transfer that does not require signature

Negotiable Instrument Act :: 1881

Coins in India are issued by :: Government of India

Currency notes in India are issued by :: Reserve Bank of India

₹1 note is issued by :: Government of India

Coinage Act :: 2011

Coins are legal tender if :: Not defaced and not below prescribed weight

Finance Act 2017 :: Cash transactions allowed below ₹2 lakh only

₹2000 note withdrawal announced in :: May 2023

₹2000 note exchange deadline :: 30 September 2023

E-Kuber :: Core Banking Solution of RBI

Digital payments regulated under :: Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007

IMPS :: Immediate Payment Service

IMPS is managed by :: NPCI

RTGS :: Real-Time Gross Settlement

RTGS is maintained by :: RBI

NEFT :: National Electronic Funds Transfer

NEFT works 24×7 since :: December 2019

NPCI :: National Payments Corporation of India

NPCI is a Section 8 company :: Not-for-Profit

UPI launch year :: 2016

BHIM launch year :: 2016

RuPay :: India’s indigenous card payment network

UPI123Pay launched in :: 2022

UPI Lite :: Offline UPI wallet for small payments

UPI Lite transaction limit :: ₹200

UPI Lite wallet limit :: ₹2,000
