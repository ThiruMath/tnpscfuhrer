  

> T.N. govt. plans to borrow ₹37,000 crores

- According to the indicative calendar of market borrowings released by the RBI the Tamil Nadu government plans to borrow ₹37,000 crore in the fourth quarter of fiscal 2023-24.
- TN has remained the top borrower in the recent years.
- State Development Loans (SDLs) form a major component of Tamil Nadu's outstanding liability.

> Tamil Nadu Coir Policy 2024

- The Tamil Nadu Coir Policy 2024 which was unveiled by the Chief Minister M.K. Stalin recently.
- It aims to attract investments worth `3,000 crore in the sector by 2030 and generate 60,000 jobs.

Key highlights

- Setting up of Centres of Excellence exclusively for new, innovative coir products.
- To establishing a coir park and a coir test laboratory.
- To promote entrepreneurs from marginalised sections
- To promote and facilitate green initiatives
- To provide a thrust for the scaling up and diversification of coir MSMEs.

> STEPS survey

- Tamil Nadu **STEPS Survey - 2** to estimate prevalence of Non Communicable Diseases (NCD) risk factors has started across the state.
- STEP wise approach to NCD risk factor surveillance (STEPS) is a simple, standardised method for collecting, analysing, and disseminating data on key NCD risk factors.
- The survey is funded by the World Bank.

> Pilot study to determine population of Nilgiri Tahr

- The Tamil Nadu government has concluded a pilot study to determine the method to estimate the population of Nilgiri Tahr.
- The study was launched as the part of Project Nilgiri Tahr.
- According to 2015 report of WWF India 3,122 Nilgiri Tahrs spread over 5,790 sq km in Tamil Nadu and Kerala.
- Project Nilgiri Tahr was launched in October 12, 2023.

> PICME software upgraded

- The Pregnancy and Infant Cohort Monitoring and Evaluation (PICME) software has been upgraded recently as PICME 3.0.
- The new PICME 3.0 will promote self - registration for RCH (Reproductive and Child Health) ID, and share the data through PICME
- with the Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS) scheme for monitoring of babies discharged from the Special New born Care Units (SNCU).
- The software captures the details of nearly 11 lakh pregnant women in the State every year.

> Tamil Nadu Coastal Restoration Mission

- The TN government has issued orders to implement the Tamil Nadu Coastal Restoration Mission.
- The mission will be implemented over the next five years at a cost of `1,675 crore in 14 coastal districts with the support of World Bank.
- The mission has four focus areas:
    1. Enhancing coastal biodiversity
    2. Coastal protection
    3. Improving livelihood through the creation of blue flag beaches and sustainable tourism
    4. Pollution abatement.
- Tamil Nadu Blue Carbon Agency will be established as a special purpose vehicle.

> Organ donations increase in T.N.

- According to the Health & Family Welfare Department of Tamil Nadu, there is a 14% increase in the number of organ donations in Tamil Nadu in 2023 when compared with 2022.
- The number of deceased donors rose from 156 in 2022 to 178 in 2023 owing to increased awareness on organ donations.
- Tamil Nadu received the Best State Award for the highest number of donations in 2022.

> Olive Ridley nesting season

- Olive Ridley sea turtles have begun nesting along the coasts of Chennai and Chengalpattu.
- The Bay of Bengal coast is one of the major nesting sites for Olive Ridleys.
- The famed mass nesting or arribadas occurs on the Odisha coast, Tamil Nadu and Andhra Pradesh coasts.
- Each Olive Ridley found and a total of 38,721 turtle lays about 100 eggs which take around 45 to 60 days to hatch.
