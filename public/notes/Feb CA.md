- The scheme would support the ‘Makkalai Thedi Maruthuvam’ (Doorstep Healthcare Scheme) scheme by bringing the facilities of diagnostic labs to the public.

> **Road accidents see an upward trend in TN**

- According to the data from the State Crime Records Bureau (SCRB) a total of 18,074 persons died in 66,841 road accidents reported in 2023 in Tamil Nadu.
- In terms of the total number of accidents in 2023,
    - Chennai and Coimbatore cities are at the top with 3,642 accidents each.
    - Chengalpattu followed with 3,387 accidents.

> **T.N. notifies land pooling scheme rules**

- The Tamil Nadu government has recently notified the Tamil Nadu Land Pooling Area Development Scheme Rules, 2024.
- Tamil Nadu Land Pooling Area Development Scheme provides for pooling together land owned by individual or individuals and developing it by the appropriate planning authority.

> **Pudhumai Penn Thittam**

- The Governor’s policy address which was written by the Tamil Nadu government highlights that 2.73 lakh girl students are benefiting every month under the Pudhumai Penn scheme in in 2023-24.
- As a result of this initiative there has been an increase of 34 percent in the enrolment of girl students from government schools into colleges during 2023-24.
- The scheme aims to provide a monthly cash assistance of `1,000 to all girl students pursuing higher education, who have completed their studies in government-run schools from Class 6 to 12.

> **Two Resolutions passed**

- The Tamil Nadu Assembly unanimously adopted two resolutions against the ‘One nation, One Election’ proposal and the proposed delimitation after 2026.
- Reasons for the resolution include,
- ‘One Nation, One Election’ policy is impractical, and goes against the fundamentals of the Constitution
- The delimitation proposed after 2026 would reduce the representation of the southern States, especially Tamil Nadu

> **Maghizhchi Programme**

- Maghizhchi Programme was launched recently by the Director General of Police Shankar Jiwal in Madurai.
- It is a mental well-being programme for police personnel and their families.

> **Sale of cotton candy banned**

- Sale of cotton candy banned in Tamil Nadu after samples reveal presence of industrial dye
- Rhodamine-B.
- It was found that Rhodamine-B was used as a colouring agent in cotton candies.

> **Tamil Nadu State Policy for Women 2024**

- Chief Minister M.K. Stalin unveiled the Tamil Nadu State Policy for Women 2024 recently.
- **Vision** - To ensure social justice for all and provide women access to their rightful entitlements.

**Objective**

- To establish a gender sensitive education system and reduce the dropout rate among girls.
- To improve health and nutrition status of adolescent girls and women.
- To increasing their participation in the workplace.
- To promote digital literacy to bridge the digital gender gap.
- To support women for remunerative placements.
- To encourage participation of women in politics.

- A high-level women empowerment committee headed by the Chief Secretary shall meet once in six months to review the implementation of the policy.
- The policy is valid for 10 years from the date of its notification or till a new policy is formulated.

> **Tamil Nadu State Highways Authority**

- The Bill to establish Tamil Nadu State Highways Authority (TNSHA) was passed in the ongoing Budget session.
- It is a statutory body to implement road infrastructure projects in Tamil Nadu.
- TNSHA will be formed on the lines of the National Highways Authority of India (NHAI) to maintain and manage roads as per global standards.

> **Report on Keeladi excavation**

- The Centre told the Madurai Bench of the Madras High Court that the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) will publish the report on the first two phases of Keeladi archaeological excavations in Sivaganga district.
- Mr. Ramakrishna had led the first two phases of the excavations in Keeladi between 2014 and2016.
- There were no significant findings in Phase III of the excavations.
- The Tamil Nadu State Department of Archaeology carried out the excavations from the fourth phase and so far nine phases had been completed.
